SCORING



  • A team will score a point:
    • by successfully landing the ball on the opponentâ€™s court.
    • when the opposing team commits a fault.
    • when the opposing team receives a penalty.


  • Faults:
    • If two or more faults happen, the first one is counted. If the faults happen at the same time, the rally is replayed and no team scores.


  • Rally
    • The sequence of playing actions from when a player serves the ball until the ball is out of play.
    • If the serving team wins a rally, they continue to serve until a fault happens, but if the receiving team wins a rally, they will serve next.




  • Front zone:
    • This is the section between the center axis and the attack line.


  • A match can consist of 5 sets. A set is won when a team scores 25 points with a minimum lead of 2 points. So if the game is tied at 24-24, one team will need to score 2 additional points to win the game (26-24, 28-26, etc.).
    • A team needs to win 3 sets to win a match. If there is a 2-2 tie, the 5th set is played to 15 points instead of 25. The minimum lead of 2 points still applies here.
