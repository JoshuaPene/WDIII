PASSING



Passing seems to be an underrated aspect of volleyball. Passing is extremely difficult and is a necessary part of setting up an attack. If you don't know how to pass well... you might as well figure it out or else your teammates might exile you from their team. Just kidding, but passing is for real a huge deal. In the video and steps below, I will give you some instructions and tips that can improve your passing game. The video below is a how to guide that explains passing in steps, but if you don't want to watch it, keep scrolling and there will be instructions for you to follow (just know, everything will be explained for right-handed hitters; but, if you are not right-handed, just switch the steps presented to you to the left instead of right)!







    Stance
    In order to pass well, start with your foundation
  • Your feet should be shoulder-width apart
  • Have both feet staggered so that you are ready to move easily and you aren't balanced awkwardly
  • Have your knees bent
  • Have your arms in front of you and keep them relaxed so you can be ready to receive all the time


    • Platform
    You need to create a solid platform for the ball to land on
  • Your fingers need to be locked together
  • You should have your thumbs parellel to one another
  • Lock your elbows away from your body


    • Direction and Contact
    It also helps to know where you're passing to
  • Where you step determines where the ball is going to be passed
  • Begin your contact motion with your hands at your knee level
  • Contact the ball at your waist so bring your arms up from your knees to hit the ball
  • Finish your contact motion at your sternum
