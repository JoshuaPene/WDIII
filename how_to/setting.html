SETTING



Setting is an extremely rewarding aspect of volleyball... when it's executed well. Setting allows you extreme control of the ball and where it will be placed. Learning how to control the ball through setting is a task that takes dilligence and practice to master. Hopefully, this video and instructions will help you learn aspects of proper setting. The video below is a how to guide that explains passing in steps, but if you don't want to watch it, keep scrolling and there will be instructions for you to follow (just know, everything will be explained for right-handed hitters; but, if you are not right-handed, just switch thesteps presented to you to the left instead of right)!







    Posture
    Setting requires a proper posture in order for a proper pass
  • Your feet should be shoulder-width apart
  • Put your right foot in front to help the direction of your pass
  • Have your knees bent and have a straight back


    • Handwork
    You need to have a flexbile platform for the ball to land in
  • Flex back your wrists/fingers so you are ready to meet the ball
  • Thumbs facing each other 2 inches apart (basically, make a triangle out of your hands)
  • Receive the ball on your hairline
  • Power comes from legs, elbows, and your wrist so extend all 3 of those to power through the ball


    • Control
    Now that you can set, learn to control it
  • Set with your fingertips, not your fingers
  • Get every finger on the ball for control and balance
  • Have your hands go open and splash away from you
