SERVING



There are many technical tweaks that go into serving a volleyball to the point where it is very daunting; however, through these instructions you will be able to break serving down to a science that you can understand. It starts with your stance and approach to the the ball and ends with your hit. The video below is a how to guide that explains serving in steps, but if you don't want to watch it, keep scrolling and there will be instructions for you to follow (just know, everything will be explained for right-handed hitters; but, if you are not right-handed, just switch the steps presented to you to the left instead of right)!







    Stance

  • Begin feet positioned in a “v” or “l” shape depending on how comfortable the positions are for you
  • Left foot facing the net, right foot 45 to 90 degrees away from left foot
  • Have an open stance (feet shoulder width apart)
  • Put weight on rear hip (right hip) that way our momentum will power the ball when you move
  • Chest 45 to 90 degrees at the net


    • Tossing

  • Present the ball to the hitting shoulder when you toss it
  • Throw the ball so it will land to the right of your left foot once you step (eventually)
  • Don't lean back or forward too far to hit the ball
  • You want the ball to be about a foot above your highest contact point
  • The lower the toss, the easier it is to contact
  • Minimal motion and as little spin on ball as possible (unless you try more advanced serves, but these are the basics for now)


    • Hitting

  • Make sure your arm is ready to hit (get your arm beside your head once you toss the ball)
  • You want your hand to be open and firm
  • Rotate your body with your swing to maximize your power going into the ball
  • Swing through the ball and don't stop once you hit it
  • Extend your elbow and arm out from behind your head into the ball










    • Power Notes
    Your power comes from:
  • Transferring momentum
  • Body rotation
  • Arm speed


    • Starting position
  • Feet V or L shape
  • Weight back
  • Present ball
  • Elbow back (make sure your arm is ready to hit above your head once you toss the ball)
  • Big, open hand


    • Serving motion (4 steps)
    Toss:
  • Over to your hitting shoulder (will be talking over right handed serve)
    • Step:
  • Same foot as side you threw the ball up from
    • Elbow:
  • Goes behind back for momentum and force on swing
    • Freeze:
  • Follow through and stop for accuracy


    • Spin serve
    Adding spin to your serve will make it way harder to defend
  • On your toss, throw the ball to where it has top spin aiming at the net
  • Snap your wrist once you hit the ball instead of just hitting it normally




    • Float serve
    This one's strictly for the big bois
  • You want to have no spin on your toss (the less spin on the ball, the more it will wobble in air making it less predictable)
  • Make hand very firm, not soft at all and fingers not spread too much
  • Wrist straight and hand as firm as possible
  • Contact ball at the base of your middle finger
  • Listen for more of a thud than a slap once you hit it
  • Aim for the second or third red/white on the poles
