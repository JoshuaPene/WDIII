BLOCKING



There's nothing more satisfying than receiving the perfect pass for a spike and making contact on the ball so well it would make vetereans cry... oh wait, yeah there is. It's stuffing that perfect spike back in an attackers face. Blokcing is a necessary part of volleyball that, if done well, can score you quite a few points. The video below is a how to guide that explains blocking, but if you don't want to watch it, keep scrolling and there will be instructions for you to follow (just know, everything will be explained for right-handed hitters; but, if you are not right-handed, just switch thesteps presented to you to the left instead of right)!







    POSTURE

    You need to be prepared to ruin a spiker's day
  • Your feet should be shoulder-width apart
  • Have your knees bent
  • Have your shoulders in front to make sure that you are leaning slightly forward
  • Put your hands in front of you so it will help you gauge the distance between you and the net
  • Always land with arms in front of you to avoid the net and always land softly with both feet in a squat so as to cushion the force and protect your knees
  • Reach over, not up
