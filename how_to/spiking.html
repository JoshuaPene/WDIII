SPIKING



Spiking is a vital part of volleyball. It is a sure way to crush your opponent leaving them wondering what they've done to deserve such harsh treatment. Now spiking is not an easy task at all, but in order to understand how to execute a spike, I will break it down into footwork and arm swining mechanics to make things more simple. The video below is a how to guide that explains spiking in steps, but if you don't want to watch it, keep scrolling and there will be instructions for you to follow (just know, everything will be explained for right-handed hitters; but, if you are not right-handed, just switch the steps presented to you to the left instead of right)!







    FOOTWORK

    Overview
  • 3 step process for footwork
  • (Right-hander) Start with the left foot back
  • The order of steps to the net should be left-right-left
  • The length of steps should be medium, long, short and the rhythm of your steps should be slow to fast
  • Swing your arms from behind you up to build momentum
  • Lead your hit with the opposite arm of your hitting arm to increase momentum in the body






    • Step 1

  • Aim body facing the net
  • Begin with your arms at your side, but start swinging them behind your body to get momentum in your jump
  • Begin around the mid-line on your side of the court
  • This should be a medium-sized step with your left foot

    • Step 2

  • Turn your foot 45 degrees from the net (basically make it parallel, but don’t snap your ankle.
  • This step should be a long step with your right foor
  • Swing your arms behind your back


    • Step 3

  • Take a short step angled parallel to the net
  • Your foot should be aimed 90 degrees parallel to the net
  • Swing your arms up in position to spike
  • Make sure when you start to jump, you jump upwards and not forwards into the net.




    • ARM MECHANICS

    Overview
  • 3 step process for arm swinging
  • You will need to have an open hand and upon hitting the ball
  • Relax your arm as much as possible when hitting and hit on top of the ball when possible






    • Step 1

  • It may help to think of this position as the "Zeus" position
  • You want to have your hitting arm's elbow bent, wrist straight, and hand open
  • Use your opther arm as a leading arm that you can pull down on to generate momentum

    • Step 2

  • Put your elbow as close to your ear as possible
  • Begin bringing your leading arm down so your momentum begins going through the ball


    • Step 3

  • Your arm should be as relaxed as possible when you hit the ball down
  • Make sure you hit the ball with your elbow up high because this will make your ball hit downwards faster
  • You will open up away from your intended target, rotate to generate power, and begin aiming to your target
  • Your left arm should follow down to your side or you should use it to catch your stomach so you don’t over-rotate
  • Snap your wrist when you hit the ball to create topspin










    • Generating topspin
    Now with the basiscs down, you want to be sure your attack is lethal
  • You want to have your hand as relaxed as possible and you want it to be open
  • Make sure every part of your hand contacts the ball
  • Contact the ball at the base of your middle finger
  • Snap your wrist (with a relaxed hand) on the ball to generate spin
