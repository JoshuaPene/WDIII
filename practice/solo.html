ONE HOUR SOLO PRACTICE









    Start
    (2-5 Minutes)
  • Begin practicing by getting your body warmed up. Jog a lap, stretch, do some quick jumps; do anything that gets your heart pumping a bit!
  • Find a wall that will be your friend for the next hour






    • Serving
    (10-15 Minutes)
  • You can practice serving however you’d like depending on where you are practicing, but I would recommend serving into a wall. If possible, mark on the wall how high the net will be that you are playing on. Try to hit above that line and be consistent.
  • You can also serve over a net, but chasing your ball down will be annoying over time, so just choose what works best for you.
  • Take the time to focus on you serving technique and don’t just rush through this section.






    • Passing
    (10-15 Minutes)
  • Throw a ball into a wall and receive the ball off the rebound. I recommend putting a box (water bottle, etc.) on the ground near the wall and pass to the box.
  • Throw the ball at various heights, speeds, and angles in order to practice your agility and passing accuracy.






    • Setting
    (10-15 Minutes)
  • Find a wall and put a mark on the wall where the top of the net would be. You will set the ball off the wall to the top of the line where the net is.
  • Try not to go over the line, but just reach barely above it. That will be a good spot for a spiker to receive the ball.
  • Keep doing this and vary the angle at which you are throwing the ball off the wall. Also, practice back setting and jump setting if you feel like you know-how.






    • Spiking
    (10-15 Minutes)
  • Similar to serving, you will be hitting into a wall; however, step about 10-15 feet away from the wall.
  • You will want to spike the ball above the net line and focus on the fundamentals of spiking.
  • Practice by varying your body positions. Aim your body as if you are hitting a cross-shot or a line-shot. You can do this and still have the ball come back to you if you hit it correctly.
