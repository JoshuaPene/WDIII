ONE HOUR TEAM PRACTICE









    Warm Up
    (2-5 Minutes)
  • (2-5 minutes) Begin practicing by getting your body warmed up. Jog a lap, stretch, do some quick jumps; do anything that gets your heart pumping a bit!
  • (2-5 minutes) Give a rundown of the practice to others.
  • (5-10 minutes) Play a downball game!


    • Downball
  • 5 people on each side of the court.
  • You will line up in a semi-circle line opened up to the net. (three people in the back row and two in the front row. Nobody will be in the setter position).
  • Your goal is to successfully perform a back row attack (having the back row spike behind the midline).
  • Once you hit a back-row attack, your team on your side will quickly rotate (rotate in a circle so everyone can try every position available).






    • Serving Progressions
    (10 Minutes)
  • Serve with a partner from the backline.
  • If there is a coach or experienced player, have them critique and help others serving.






    • Serving Competition
    (10 Minutes)
  • Take your players and divide them into two teams
  • Set up 10 cones on one side of the court. 5 of the cones will be for one team, and the other 5 for the other team.
  • Put one player from each team on top of their cones (all this is doing is creating a target for servers to hit)
  • You will have the rest of the players serve from the backline at the people in the cones.
  • Have teams take turns serving at the cones.
  • The goal is to serve to each cone until you have made it to each one.
  • Every time a cone is hit at, have the players who are receiving, switch with the servers so everyone can practice aiming and serving.
  • Do this until there is a winner, switch up teams, and repeat until satisfied.






    • Hitting lines vs defense
    (10 Minutes)
  • Divide the teams to each side of the court. Roughly three people should be on the “hitting side” and everyone else should go to the “defense side.”
  • The attacking side will set and then spike to the defenders.
  • The defender's goal is to receive the ball and then send an attack back to the hitting side.
  • Once the defenders hit the ball back, reset the drill and perform it again. Switch positions where needed and tweak where necessary.






    • Scrimmage or Queen/King of the Court
    (10-15 Minutes)
  • A scrimmage is a little slow, so if you want a more fast-paced game with many variations, consider Queen/King of the Court


    • Queen/King of the Court
  • Queen/King of the court is an awesome game where you split your players into teams of three.
  • Once split into teams, play a normal rally.
  • Play with normal rules until one team loses. Once there is a winner, have the losing team switch out and get replaced by another team that is waiting.
  • Keep track of points if you want and declare a winner after 7 points and switch teams up.






    • Wrap up
    (5 Minutes)
  • Depending on your situation, leave these five minutes to wrap up as necessary.
